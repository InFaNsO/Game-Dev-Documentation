# Automation — World & Setting

> The fiction the automation game sits in. **Locked 2026-06-08.** Like every game in the portfolio, it shares the **Mani world** but is a **different era with no plot connection** — callbacks only.

---

## Era — LOCKED: pre-Breach Mani Accord golden age, during a *frontier boom*

The shop needs a living economy, mage customers, refined Mani, and competitors — that only exists in a **thriving Mani civilization**, which places this **pre-Breach**, at the height of the Mani Accord.

The specific framing is a **frontier-expansion boom**: the Accord is pushing into wild, Mani-rich, dangerous territory. This earns three things:

1. **Organic combat-spell demand.** Mages, delvers, and guards pushing the frontier *need* spell-gear to survive. That's *why* a peacetime civilization buys combat spells — and it rhymes with the dream game's colonization and the city pillar's "tame the biome."
2. **Built-in escalation.** Deeper frontier = tougher threats = the market demands stronger **multi-Mani** spells. The loop's growth curve is justified by the fiction.
3. **Callbacks, no plot-link.** It's the doomed golden age that will later cause The Breach — optional dramatic irony for players who know the Looter Shooter. Pure callback, per the portfolio rule.

### The Akash tease (the one deliberate callback)
Periodic **endgame special requests** hint that the Accord is secretly chasing **"a new kind of Mani"** — which **never materializes**. Mechanically this is the **all-4-element combination = Akash** (see `06`): the Accord's obsession, teased but **never craftable**. *The crafting ceiling and the fiction are the same thing.* This is a quiet wink at the Akash doorway experiment / The Breach, with no plot dependency.

## The scenario — what the player does

You are an **artisan-entrepreneur running a spell-gear workshop**:

- **Design spells** — the signature *Conjuration Engineering* (see `05`/`06`).
- **Produce** them through your **workshop automation** (fully machine-automated, feeding a mega assembler — see `05`).
- **House** each spell in **gear**: gauntlet, wand, sword, armor, etc. (the delivery device a mage uses to cast).
- **Sell** to mages, delvers, and guards in a **competitive market** with **rival workshops** (see `07`).
- Win periodic **special-request bake-offs** (best submission rewarded handsomely; weak ones eat losses).
- **Grow** → unlock **multi-Mani** spells (deeper combinations for maximum power), expand the workshop, craft higher-quality equipment.

## Customers & demand

The frontier boom creates a textured customer base:
- **Mages / spellcasters** — primary buyers of offensive and utility spell-gear.
- **Delvers / explorers** — pushing into wild Mani zones; want survival + burst.
- **Guards / frontier security** — defensive and control spells.

Demand varies by **element, spell behavior, and gear type**, and shifts over time (and with competitor activity) — the market is alive, not a fixed order board.
