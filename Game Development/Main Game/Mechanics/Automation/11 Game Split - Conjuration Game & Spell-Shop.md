# Automation — Game Split: ① Conjuration Game → ② Spell-Shop

> **LOCKED 2026-06-10: the automation pillar = 2 GAMES** — one proving game for Surface A, then the full fusion. Both complete, polished, *shipped* products (ship-everything principle).

---

## Why 2 (not 1, not 3)

The ladder rule: *each game proves the riskiest still-unproven thing with the least scope, while being a complete product* — and each standalone must pass the **identity test** (it's a real game with its own soul, not "the fusion minus features").

- **Surface A is the pillar's one genuinely unproven core** — nothing like Conjuration Engineering exists (Modulus is the nearest and it's niche + flawed); the particle tech is validated on paper but the *fun* is unproven. If it isn't fun, the pillar collapses → it must be proven first, small.
- **Surface A alone passes the identity test**: a Zachlike commission-puzzle game is a complete, beloved genre at exactly this scope (Opus Magnum is the existence proof).
- **Surface B alone FAILS it**: factory-without-the-signature = a generic mini-Factorio in the most crowded indie genre — "the fusion minus its soul." The genre itself is already proven (Factorio/DSP), so a standalone proves nothing the fusion needs. → no 3rd game.
- **1 game (no split) rejected**: it would leave the riskiest mechanic unproven inside a Game-2-scale build, violating the ladder's spirit.
- **Accepted cost:** the market + competitor AI stays unproven until ② — acceptable because it's the pillar's *least* novel system (The Good Company precedent, utility AI) and ② is where it has a real factory to pressure.

---

## ① The Conjuration Game *(working title — small, ~Opus Magnum scale)*

A **Zachlike commission-puzzle game**: pure Surface A, no factory, no market, no economy.

- **Moment-to-moment:** a client letter arrives with a **spec** (element, damage type, stat thresholds — "pierce ≥ X, cast ≤ Y" — part budget, gem). Build the process on the gem grid → fire it in the **Test Room** → watch the construct conjure and launch → iterate until it passes → get **scored**.
- **Scoring:** spec-fit · part-cost · cast time · gem efficiency (the Zachlike histogram/leaderboard hooks fall out naturally).
- **Structure:** ~30–50 authored commissions arranged **along the element matrix** (base → pairs → triples), light frontier-flavor wrapper, **the forbidden all-4 Akash commission as the finale tease** + a free **sandbox mode**.
- **What it proves/builds (all carries forward unchanged):**
  - the **#1 gray-box** — assemble process → conjure → fire → *does it feel good?*
  - the **entire VFX template library** (~8 templates, `06`/`09`)
  - the **module sim** + the **derived-stat model** (incl. cast time)
  - the **spec-judging code** — which literally becomes ②'s bake-off engine
- Mirrors **Game 1 (Parry Combat)** exactly: the pillar's riskiest identity mechanic, wrapped small and shipped.

## ② The Spell-Shop *(the fusion — the full `00`–`10` design)*

Adds everything else on top of ①'s core: **Surface B** (warehouse factory + mega assembler), the **living market + competitor AI v1**, the **two-currency progression** (Money/RP/reputation), **gear**, **plot**. Reuses ① additively — conjuration sim, Test Room, VFX library, and the spec-judge (now the bake-off engine).
