# City Builder — Production & Economy (Phase 2)

> The economic heart: population, needs, production chains, and how Mani threads through everything. Designed via the **"10% original" rule** — a familiar Anno chassis (90%) made distinctive by the Mani framing (10%). Core locked 2026-06-07; specific goods/numbers are reference targets.

---

## The 90% / 10% split (the design lens)

- **90% familiar (chassis):** Anno's **tiered population** + **multi-step production chains** (raw → intermediate → finished). Proven, readable, responsive — not reinvented.
- **10% original (the twist):** **magic is the plumbing and the appliances.** Mani is the literal **utility grid + manufacturing input + defense fuel + (dream-game) spells** — *one substance, elemental variants, every system.* Plus **refining as the civilization's core craft.** The originality is the *framing*, NOT the elements (elemental magic is the well-trodden part we deliberately don't lean on).

## 2.1 Population — tiered (LOCKED) — 3 tiers + Akash capstone

**Anno-style escalating tiers.** Each tier unlocks by satisfying the previous tier's needs, demands more complex goods, and provides more workforce/income. Chosen for being responsive + easy to understand. **"Expand a building → more output"** (Anno) slots in as tier-upgrades.

**3 population tiers, shared across all species** (NOT separate per-species ladders — species differentiate by demand *bias*, not separate tiers). Each tier's *signature* new good escalates in Mani-element complexity:

| Tier | Pop (flavor) | Signature good | Needs | Wants |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **T1** | Settlers | **1-element** | 2 | 1 |
| **T2** | Citizens | **2-element compound** | 3 | 1 |
| **T3** | Artisans | **3-element** | 3 | 2 |

- **Higher tiers keep the lower needs** (cumulative, Anno-style) + add the new signature good.
- **The knock-on (the point):** a 3-element T3 good can't be made from one biome → **population advancement = forced multi-biome expansion.** Tier progression, era progression, and logistics scope all advance on **one axis.**
- Counts are **reference targets** (tune in playtest); the *relationships* are locked. ~8 need-goods + ~4 wants total — bounded.

### Akash Synthesis = the endgame capstone (LOCKED 2026-06-07) — NOT a 4th population tier
The all-4-element stage is **a great-work / monument you construct**, sitting *above* the tier ladder — **not** a population class (a 4th tier would be a hollow demand-sink, since reaching it triggers the endgame). The **Akash Synthesis** requires **all 4 refined elements** (→ you must control all 4 biomes + a humming T3 economy) → **building it triggers the endgame sequence.** Expresses Akash's "synthesized capstone achievement" identity far better than a recurring demand, and is more dramatic (a climactic Great Synthesis = the win condition).

### Wants vs Needs (LOCKED)
- **Needs** = materialistic, *consumed* goods/utilities (Mani-Stove, water, food). **Hard** — unmet → unhappiness / decline / won't advance.
- **Wants** = decorative, **Town-to-City-style**. **Soft** — met → happiness bonus (faster growth / income / advancement), optional. The decoration-tied-to-happiness layer (natural home for free-placed cozy building).
- **Species bias is cheap:** mostly *shared* need-goods with a **quantity bias** (Amphibian→more Jal-water, Reptile→more Agni-warmth, Avian→more Vayu-air, Human→balanced) **+ ~1 species-flavored good per tier.** NOT four separate good-sets — this is what stops 4 species quadrupling content.

## 2.2 Needs — a mix, unified through Mani (LOCKED)

| Need layer | Met by | How Mani shows up |
|---|---|---|
| **Survival** | Direct refined-Mani **utilities** (the "grid") | Agni warmth · Jal water · Vayu air · Bhu shelter — piped to homes |
| **Comfort** | **Mani-tech goods** (manufactured appliances) | Appliance = material body + refined Mani (Mani-Stove, Jal-Cooler, Vayu-Loom…) |
| **Luxury** | **Multi-element Mani-tech** + high-tier goods | Appliances needing *several* refined elements + processed goods |

**Species twist:** each species biases its needs toward its element (Amphibian→more Jal, Reptile→more Agni, Avian→more Vayu, Human→balanced). The Main City (all four species) needs a **broad** portfolio; a single-species satellite optimizes **one** → that asymmetry is *why* you trade.

## 2.3 Production-chain shape (LOCKED) — two interlocking chain types

**Principle: classic chains make the *bodies/materials*; Mani provides the *function*.** They interlock — classic chains feed Mani-tech goods.

### Mani chains (the signature)
**Core loop:** harvest **lots of raw Mani** → **refine** → split refined Mani two ways: **(a)** pipe to homes as utilities, **(b)** feed manufacturing for Mani-tech goods. Refining is the central bottleneck everything flows through.
`Raw Agni Mani (mined) → Refinery → Refined Agni → [piped as warmth utility] OR [Workshop + Metal → Mani-Stove]`

### Classic chains (the 90% backbone — bounded ~5)
| Chain | Steps | Feeds | Mani touch |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Timber** | Forestry → Sawmill → Planks | Construction, furniture, tools | — |
| **Metal** | Mine ore → Smelter → Metal | Appliances, tools, turrets, construction | Smelter runs on **Agni** |
| **Food** | Fields → Mill/Kitchen → Processed food | Survival + comfort food | Fields boosted by **Bhu/Jal** |
| **Textiles** | Fiber → Weavery → Cloth → Clothing | Comfort | Weavery uses **Vayu** (looms) |
| **Glass & Ceramics** | Sand/clay → Kiln → Glass/containers | Windows, appliance parts, **Akash lenses** | Kiln runs on **Agni** |
| *Intermediates* | Tools (metal+planks) · Bricks (construction) | Industry / building | — |

→ Even the "regular" economy pulls on Mani (smelters/kilns burn Agni; farms boosted by Bhu/Jal). **Mani stays load-bearing everywhere.**

### The killer systemic hook
**Higher-tier Mani-tech goods require MULTIPLE refined elements** (Climate Cabinet = Jal + Agni; transit-lift = Vayu + Bhu). You **cannot** build them from one biome → **forced inter-biome trade.** This single rule unifies the pillars: **needs drive production → production demands a multi-element portfolio → the portfolio forces logistics.**

**Example interlocked recipes:**
- `Mani-Stove = Metal + Refined Agni + assembly` (comfort)
- `Jal-Cooler = Metal + Glass + Refined Jal` (comfort, two chains + Mani)
- `Climate Cabinet = Metal + Glass + Refined Jal + Refined Agni` (luxury, multi-biome)

## 2.4 Refining = a production *building* (LOCKED)

Refining is a **scalable, automatable production building** with depth via **efficiency / quality / throughput upgrades** (Anno "expand → more output") — **NOT** a per-instance manual minigame like the Looter Shooter. City scale wants automation, not manual minigames. *(Relationship to the LS refining minigame + dream-game fusion: flagged for the cross-game reconciliation.)*

## 2.5 Building expansion — capacity scales via Mani (LOCKED logic 2026-06-07; numbers TBD)

Anno's **"expand a building → more output,"** wired into the Mani economy: **a building's capacity ceiling is unlocked in steps, each step gated by feeding it a specific, thematically-matched refined Mani element.** This makes **Mani the building-upgrade currency** (on top of utility-input + goods-input) and is *another* driver of multi-element trade.

**Example — Farm (food):** base **10** tiles (Mani-free) → **+Bhu** expansion (soil/fertility) → **20** tiles → **+Jal** expansion (irrigation) → **40** tiles. *(Numbers are placeholders; the LOGIC is locked: low Mani-free base → each thematically-matched elemental socket multiplies the working-tile capacity → ceiling.)*

- **Thematic match (LOCKED):** the elements that expand a building fit its function — Farm = **Bhu** (soil) + **Jal** (water); Smelter = **Agni**; etc. Each building has a short **elemental expansion path**. This *also* means a building can't be maxed from one biome → reinforces inter-biome trade.
- **OPEN sub-decision — upgrade cost model:** **(a) one-time** material/Mani cost to build the expansion (simple, Anno-like) vs **(b) ongoing Mani feed** to *sustain* the higher capacity (the building is "plugged into" continuous Bhu/Jal supply, like an appliance on a power grid — lose the feed → capacity throttles back). **Rec: (b), likely a hybrid** (one-time build cost + ongoing feed to run at capacity) — it fits the Mani-as-infrastructure theme and wires building output into the **logistics supply tension** (sever a route → the refined-Mani feed dries up → buildings throttle back down toward their Mani-free base). *Decide in Phase 3 / playtest.*

## 2.6 Money & economy (LOCKED 2026-06-07) — Anno model + light lever

**Anno-style money balance** (NOT a Cities:Skylines budget-sim). Income = **passive tax** from happy households (scales with tier × happiness) + **trade income** from selling surplus Mani/goods; expenses = **building upkeep** (+ the ongoing-Mani-feed from §2.5). Net balance; running dry stalls growth. Plus **one light tax↔happiness lever** — raise levies for more income at a happiness cost (the "greed vs happiness" knob the references value). Deep market / dynamic-pricing / policy-slider simulation = **OUT** (see `08`).
