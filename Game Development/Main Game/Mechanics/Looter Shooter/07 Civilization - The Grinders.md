
### The Grinders - Brief

The Grinders are the **sole human faction** in the current era — the last remnants of the [[14 Naming Glossary#The Federation|Mani Accord's]] human working caste, who survived [[12 The Akashic and The Bleed#2 Timeline|The Breach]] in the Lithic Mow valley and degraded over millennia from federation citizens into hunter-gatherers. A small community of ~15–20 people who live in and around the silent drill of [[05 Mega Structures - Game Worlds#Lithic Mow|Lithic Mow]]. They are mining-proficient, **Mani-ignorant**, and **semi-hostile to the player by default** — wary of strangers, slow to trust.

> Full archetypes and enemy roster: [[11 Factions and Species#Grinders (Humans — primary prototype faction)|Factions doc]]. Trust-arc design: [[15 Grinder Trust Arc]].

---

### Pre-Breach Heritage

The Grinders' ancestors were not leaders. They were **Accord workers** — miners, technicians, ore-haulers at Lithic Mow. They did not know about the inner-circle Akash conspiracy. They were not even the first to suffer when The Breach happened (the Amphibians and Reptiles, bio-attuned, took the worst). Most of the Lithic Mow workers were physically far enough from the Vats event to survive. They watched the world end from a mountain valley and inherited a drill they could not operate.

This explains their character today:
- **Mining-proficient** — passed down through generations as the one viable skill
- **Wary of authority** — their grandparents' grandparents trusted the Accord and were betrayed
- **Mani-ignorant** — refinement knowledge died with the Accord's specialists; only oral fragments survived

---

### Present-Day Life

A tribal community of hunter-gatherers and miners. They build with scavenged Accord materials, eat what the valley provides, and use raw Mani ore as crude grenades — often catastrophically. They have lost relatives to their own Mani throws. **They are desperate, even if they don't admit it, for knowledge that would let them stop dying to ignorance.**

The Grinders maintain a loose tribal structure with a **Chief** (pragmatic leader), a **Shaman** (most invested in Mani lore), and various **Scouts** and **Drillers** filling defensive and labor roles. Conflicts within the tribe are resolved through council; outsiders are kept at arm's length.

---

### Disposition Toward the Player

**Wary, not hostile.** The player wakes near the Lithic Mow valley as an obvious outsider — clothing too clean, knowledge too strange. The Grinders watch before engaging. Initial encounters set the test:

- Save a tribe member in need → opening earned
- Demonstrate practical usefulness → camp access provisional
- Reveal Mani refinement knowledge → trust transformed
- Reclaim Lithic Mow together → tribal kin

> Detailed beats in [[15 Grinder Trust Arc]].

The trust arc is the prototype's primary social mechanic. Once allied, the Grinders become the player's base community — providing companions, vendors, crafters, and the labor force needed to expand operations downriver to Genesis Vats.

---

### Why the Player Matters to the Grinders

The Grinders don't need a savior. They need a **teacher.** The player is the last carrier of pre-Breach refinement knowledge. Teaching the Grinders to make the first Bhu-Mani — using equipment at the Headwaters Accord outpost the Grinders had dismissed as scrap — is the moment the relationship transforms. After that beat, **the Grinders are not the player's followers. They are the player's first colleagues in rebuilding the world.**

### Enemy Archetypes (combat-tier)

When trust is not yet established, the Grinders are encountered as combat enemies in territorial disputes. Even once allied, rival splinter-Grinder groups (rejecting the player's knowledge as heresy) may appear as antagonists for side quests.
