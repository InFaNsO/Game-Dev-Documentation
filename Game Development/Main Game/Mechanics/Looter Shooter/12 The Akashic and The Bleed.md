
# The Akashic and The Bleed

The cosmic-horror lore underlying the game's setting. Covers The Breach event, the corrupting force (The Akashic formal / The Bleed common), the source dimension (Akash), and the consequences that shape the present-day world.

> See [[14 Naming Glossary]] for pronunciations and quick-reference names. See [[11 Factions and Species]] for how this lore manifests in enemy design.

---

## 1. The Truth (revealed mid-game)

> The Mani Accord existed to refine Mani for energy. That was the cover story — known to the working castes, the public, every Amphibian vapor-worker and every Reptile heat-tender. **It was true. It was also incomplete.**
>
> The Accord's inner circle had a second goal, hidden from their own people: to refine **Akash-Mani** as a **doorway** to the dimension it came from. The substance is a crystallized sample of **Akash** itself — and the Accord wanted to travel back to the source.
>
> They succeeded at refining Akash-Mani. They built the doorway. **They opened it.** Something on the other side noticed and pushed back. The doorway collapsed inward — the crystal destroyed in the same moment it functioned — and an interdimensional shockwave punctured a wound between realities. **The Breach.**
>
> Through The Breach, **The Akashic** — radiation from Akash — poured into our world for seconds. Then The Breach sealed itself. The leak stopped. The damage was done.
>
> All Mani in the world was permanently contaminated with traces of The Akashic. The Amphibians and Reptiles, bio-attuned to Mani, absorbed enough radiation to corrupt and **immortalize** them. They became Husks. They have been wandering for millennia.
>
> Nobody alive at game-start knows the secret. The Grinders know the Accord existed and that something terrible happened. They do not know about Akash, about the doorway, about the conspiracy. **The first named-NPC purification is when the truth surfaces.**

## 2. Timeline

| Era | What happened |
|---|---|
| **Pre-Breach** (the Accord era) | Three species cooperate as the Mani Accord. Mega Structures built and operational. Mani refinement perfected for Bhu/Jal/Agni/Vayu. Inner circle secretly works toward Akash refinement and dimensional crossing. |
| **The Final Refinement** | Akash-Mani successfully refined. Doorway opened. Something pushes back. **The Breach occurs.** |
| **The Breach itself** | **Lasts only seconds.** Interdimensional shockwave radiates outward. Akash-Mani crystal destroyed in the same moment. The Akashic radiation leaks into the world. The Breach then collapses and seals itself. |
| **Immediate aftermath** | Amphibians and Reptiles caught in proximity die or become Husks. Human Accord administrators mostly die or scatter. Civilization collapses within days. |
| **The Long Recovery** (millennia) | Husks wander, immortal and unaging. Human descendants survive in scattered tribes — most knowledge lost. Mega Structures fall silent. The world *begins* to heal as The Bleed in the environment slowly dissipates (except in pooled hotspots). |
| **Game start** | Player wakes from cryo. World is post-recovery: no active Breach, no spreading corruption, but legacy contamination is everywhere. Husks still walk. Mani knowledge lost. Grinders survive in the Lithic Mow valley. |

**Critical point:** The Breach is **closed and has been for millennia.** There is no active dimensional threat in the player's timeline. The cosmic horror is in the past tense — players are dealing with consequences, not a live attack.

## 3. Why the Accord did it (motive)

Open to interpretation across audio logs, but the seed:

The Accord wasn't satisfied with refinement for energy. The inner circle — Humans primarily, with a small number of Amphibian and Reptile elites — believed Akash was a place of incomprehensible knowledge, perhaps escape from a dying universe, perhaps godhood. They built the dimensional doorway in secret because they knew the working castes would refuse to cooperate if the true plan were known. **They were right to fear that refusal. They built it anyway.**

This motivation stays partly ambiguous in audio logs — different inner-circle members give different reasons (knowledge, power, religious fulfillment, escape, pure scientific curiosity). The player constructs their own theory.

## 4. Akash — the source dimension

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What it is** | The dimension Akash-Mani is a crystallized sample of. Possibly the "substrate of reality" in the federation's cosmology (matching the Sanskrit philosophical meaning of *akasha* — ether, space, the medium of all things). |
| **Inhabitants** | **Ambiguous.** The Accord's logs suggest "something pushed back" / "we saw a movement at the threshold" / "they noticed us" — but it is never confirmed whether Akash contains intelligent beings, just hostile physics, or both. Players may believe whatever they wish. |
| **Reachable in this campaign?** | **No.** The Breach is sealed. The original Akash-Mani is destroyed. The Accord's doorway technology is gone. Akash is permanently beyond reach in this game. (Possible sequel territory.) |
| **Visible in this campaign?** | **No.** Akash is referenced only through audio logs and pre-Breach Accord documents. Never depicted. |

## 5. The Akashic / The Bleed — the corrupting force

Two-tier naming, mirroring the Mani / Panchamani structure.

| Tier | Name | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **Formal** | **The Akashic** | Mani Accord audio logs, ancient texts, optional lore codex |
| **Common** | **The Bleed** | Survivors, Grinders, vendors, gameplay UI, dialogue |

The pair carries the central metaphor: ***The Breach is the wound. The Bleed is what comes through.***

### Properties

1. **Historical residue, not active leak.** All The Akashic radiation that exists in the world today entered during the few seconds of The Breach. Nothing new is leaking.
2. **Permanently contaminates Mani.** Every grain of Mani in the world has microscopic Akashic traces. **Touching Mani touches Akash, a little.**
3. **Animates and immortalizes Husks.** Bio-attuned creatures who absorbed it during The Breach are sustained by it — they cannot die naturally because the radiation that animates them does not dissipate.
4. **Pools in environmental hotspots.** Concentrated zones around the Mega Structures (where the most Mani was being refined when The Breach happened) still carry dangerous Bleed concentrations. These are environmental hazards in-game.
5. **Refinement filters it out.** Raw Mani is *unstructured* and contains chaotic Akashic potential (random Effect Table rolls). Refined elemental Mani has structure imposed on it, making it **controllable and predictable** — with power that scales with the size of the refined piece. Refined Mani is also **safer to handle** because the Akashic content is constrained by the imposed structure.

## 6. Raw vs Refined Mani — the mechanic

This is one of the central mechanics. Locked design:

| Form | Behavior | Player use | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Raw Mani (shard)** | **Unknown structure.** Can manifest as any of the elements unpredictably. Random rolls from the **Mani Effect Table** on detonation. | Found as loot; thrown as a live-timer grenade (Bob-omb mechanic from Sparks of Hope). Auto-detonates on timer expiry. | High. Random effects can self-harm. Grinders throw raw Mani in combat and frequently injure themselves — players can bait this. |
| **Refined elemental Mani** (Bhu/Jal/Agni/Vayu) | **Structured.** Predictable, controllable single-element effects. **Power scales with size** — small shards for safe use, larger crystals for ult-tier effects. | Crafted at Mega Structures the player has reclaimed. Used in ammo, abilities, traversal gear, purification. | Lower per-use, higher per-crystal-size. A fist-sized refined Mani is dangerous to handle. |
| **Akash-Mani (refined)** | The fifth refined form. Original was destroyed at The Breach. **A new Akash-Mani is produced as the climactic act of the multi-species (good) ending** — refined safely this time, using all four elemental Manis as anchors and multi-species cooperation as the stabilizing protocol the Accord lacked. | Endgame act in the good ending only. Never wielded during gameplay; manifested as the closing beat of the campaign. | Catastrophic without proper protocol — the Accord proved it. Safe only with the head researcher's knowledge (optional Forge Husk) and multi-species cooperation. |

### Mani Effect Table (raw rolls)

Loose distribution for design reference:

- **Common (60%)**: Burn / Freeze / Push / Stagger
- **Uncommon (30%)**: Honey (root) / Vamp / Ink / Suppressed
- **Rare (10%)**: Phase (teleport caster) / Summon Mani-construct (temporary hostile) / Anti-grav lift / Create cover

## 7. The Husks (immortal prisoners)

### Two tiers (mirrors the conspiracy)

| Tier | Identity | In-game treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Common Husks** | Working-caste Amphibians and Reptiles who **did not know the secret**. The vapor-tenders, heat-workers, soldiers, citizens. | Generic enemy archetypes (Brood, Tidefall, Choir / Stalker, Speaker, Sentinel). Killable. Not story-tied. |
| **Named / Inner-Circle Husks** | Accord scientists, doorway operators, "first-through" volunteers who **knew the truth**. Each has a name and a story. | Story-gated. Purification mechanic available. Their dialogue reveals the conspiracy in layers. |

### What "killing" means narratively (generic Husks)

A common Husk has been suffering for thousands of years — neither alive nor dead, animated by foreign radiation, lucid enough to suffer, never lucid enough to rest. **Killing a common Husk severs the radiation thread that animates them.** It is mercy. The player isn't slaying monsters; they're releasing prisoners. This subtle reframe should land through audio logs, environmental storytelling, and dialogue — never lecturing the player.

### What "purification" means narratively (named Husks — forced)

Named Husks **cannot be killed** — the player must defeat them in combat *and* purify them by force. Purification uses refined elemental Mani as an anchor to **transmute the radiation in their body**, stabilizing them back into normal mortal biology. Not exorcism. **Restoration.** The purified individual remembers everything, including the centuries of half-life. They are not unbroken. But they are alive.

The forced framing is thematically load-bearing: a named Husk who has been a prisoner for millennia may not be *ready* to accept mercy — may rage against it, fear it, or beg for the suffering to continue rather than face what restoration means. The player takes their pain away regardless. *"Mercy without consent"* — consistent with the cosmic-horror tone of the setting. The Husks did not consent to their corruption; they do not get to choose to remain corrupted.

During the encounter, the named Husk transmits their specific Mani refinement knowledge **as a dying-act gift** in the moment before the forced purification — a final transmission from one researcher to another. *"Take my knowledge, then take my pain."*

## 8. Environmental Presence of The Bleed (visual / audio design)

**No Bleed model exists in the game.** The Bleed is conveyed entirely through:

- **Corrupted Husks** (direct combat encounters)
- **Bleed Zones** in the environment — pooled hotspots near Mega Structures with shader hazards (warped air, slow-floating dust, gravity-distorted debris) that apply status effects over turns
- **Audio logs** from the Accord era, sometimes spliced with strange harmonics
- **Whispers in the ambient audio** within Bleed Zones — in old federation languages, sometimes recognizable, often not
- **Post-process shader pulses** when the player picks up raw Mani or refined Mani — brief visual hint that the substance is *touching elsewhere*

This means **zero animation budget for The Bleed itself.** Shader and audio carry the entire cosmic-horror presence. Solo-dev-shippable.

## 9. The Endgame Implications

The original Akash-Mani is destroyed. There is no artifact to recover. The Breach is already sealed.

**However — a NEW Akash-Mani can be refined at endgame, correctly this time, IF the player commits to the multi-species path AND if they have learned the protocols from the optional Forge Husk** (head of Accord research, mini-boss in the Prism Forge inner sanctum side area).

**The campaign endgame is therefore not a battle against The Bleed — it is a choice about what kind of civilization to rebuild, and whether to dare reach for Akash again.** See [[13 Campaign Structure]] for the Choice mechanic.

- **Multi-species cooperation path (good ending):** Player purifies enough named Amphibians and Reptiles to seed the rebuilt federation. The three species rebuild together — slowly, carefully, with the Accord's mistakes as warning. **A new Akash-Mani is produced at endgame**, stabilized by multi-species cooperation (the protocol the Accord lacked). If the player engaged the optional Forge Husk, the refinement is clean and the rebuilt civilization reaches for the "next frontier." If they skipped, the refinement is tense and improvised but still succeeds; the epilogue is quieter. Either way: the world heals, The Bleed's pooled zones gradually dissipate, and Akash refinement passes back into civilizational knowledge — with the lesson "*never alone, never in secret*" learned in blood.
- **Humans-only path (bad ending):** Player refuses to revive the other species **and refuses to attempt Akash refinement** — even safely, even with the protocols. Humans flourish for one generation on the strength of the four elemental Manis alone. With no Amphibian or Reptile cooperation, multi-species refinement chains can never be re-established. Civilization stagnates, then declines. The last human dies alone. **The Bleed outlasts humanity. Akash refinement is lost a second time, this time by choice.**

## 10. Sequel hooks (free worldbuilding, owe nothing)

- **What was on the other side?** Never answered.
- **What did the Accord actually want from Akash?** Partially answered through audio logs; ultimately remains player interpretation.
- **What is the "next frontier"** the rebuilt civilization will reach for? Unstated. Could be controlled dimensional access, space travel, time, ascension. Sequel decides.
- **Did any cryo-sleepers besides the player survive?** Yes — encountered as recruitable NPCs along the river journey.
- **Could Akash-Mani be re-refined safely with The Accord's lessons learned?** The Accord thought so. The Accord was wrong. **The lesson of the good ending is that this question should not be asked.**
