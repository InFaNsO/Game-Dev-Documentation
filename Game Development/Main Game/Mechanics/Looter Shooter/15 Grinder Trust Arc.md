
# Grinder Trust Arc

> **Status:** Draft / in-progress. Captures the design proposal discussed in the Topic 6 session. Specific calls (number of named NPCs, refinement ceremony staging) not yet locked.

The prototype's primary social mechanic and the main game's act-one narrative spine. The player starts as a stranger to a semi-hostile tribe and ends as tribal kin sharing knowledge. The arc must feel **earned through demonstration**, not handed over via quest completion.

> See [[11 Factions and Species#Grinders (Humans — primary prototype faction)|Grinder roster]] for the four archetypes. See [[13 Campaign Structure#5 Story Acts|Act 1: Trust]] for where this fits in the campaign.

---

## 1. Design Goals

1. **The Grinders should feel like people, not quest-givers.** A small tribe (~15–20) where you remember individual faces.
2. **Trust is the gating mechanism for everything Lithic Mow has to offer** — camp access, vendor, crafting, companions.
3. **The "first refinement ceremony" is the watershed moment** — the player demonstrates pre-Breach knowledge, the Grinders are transformed, the trust tier shifts.
4. **Joint reclamation of Lithic Mow is the bonding event** — player and Grinders fight side-by-side at the Mega Structure.

---

## 2. The Five-Phase Arc

### Phase 1 — Encounter (15–30 min)
- Wake at Headwaters cryo facility. Brief tutorial in the abandoned outpost.
- Venture out. Stumble onto a lone Grinder **Scout** being attacked by Bleed-touched wildlife or a stray Husk.
- **First combat in the world** = saving the Scout. Mechanically teaches the player how combat works while narratively earning the first sliver of trust.
- Wounded Scout reluctantly leads you toward the camp.

### Phase 2 — Provisional Wary (1–2 hours)
- Enter the Grinder camp. **Chief** receives you: *"You can stay. We're watching you."*
- Limited camp access: no vendor, no crafting station yet, no quests beyond "don't die in our space."
- Witness Grinders using **raw Mani as grenades** — random effects, occasional self-harm, losing tribe members to their own ignorance.
- Handful of small "prove useful" missions: defend camp from a wildlife attack, recover stolen supplies from a nearby ruin, heal an injured Grinder.
- The **Shaman** is the most curious about the player — asks questions, watches closely. Becomes the player's social bridge into the tribe.

### Phase 3 — The First Refinement (the watershed beat)
- Shaman approaches privately: *"You know things we've lost, don't you?"*
- Player and Shaman travel back to the **Headwaters outpost** — the old Accord research site has remnant refinement equipment the Grinders dismissed as scrap.
- Player demonstrates the **first Bhu-Mani refinement** using outpost equipment + raw Mani the Shaman brought.
- Shaman is transfigured by the revelation. Brings the crystal back to camp.
- Chief calls a tribal council. **The first Bhu-Mani crystal is presented to the tribe.**
- Chief: *"Show us. All of us. We will not lose more children to ignorance."*
- **Trust tier → ALLIED.** Full camp access. Vendor opens. Crafting station opens. Refinement-teaching unlocks for Grinder colonists at the player base.

### Phase 4 — Joint Reclamation
- Player + Grinders assault the Lithic Mow drill site as a joint operation.
- Clear corruption hazards, fight any Husks/wildlife guarding the entry, reactivate the drill (or its first operational section).
- First mass-production of Bhu-Mani. Grinders celebrate openly with the player.
- **Trust tier → TRIBAL KIN.**

### Phase 5 — Beyond
- Some named Grinders become **companions / colonists** at the player base.
- Shaman becomes the player's wisdom-source for Mani lore.
- Chief becomes the political ally for outward expansion (downriver to Vats).
- Tribe supports the player's expedition.

---

## 3. Trust Tiers — three discrete levels

Story-gated rather than reputation-numerical. Three discrete moments raise the tier — no Skyrim-style grind.

| Tier | Unlocks | How to reach |
|---|---|---|
| **Wary** (start) | Camp access, basic dialogue, "prove useful" mini-quests | Save the Scout in Phase 1 |
| **Allied** | Full camp services (vendor, crafting, blueprints), recruitment of generic Grinder colonists | First refinement ceremony (end of Phase 3) |
| **Tribal Kin** | Companions in combat, Grinder lore reveals (tribe's oral history of The Breach), full base co-building, Grinder gear unlocks | Reclamation of Lithic Mow (end of Phase 4) |

## 4. Named Grinder NPCs (proposed — not locked)

The Grinder tribe is ~15–20 strong. Of those, **3 named individuals** carry the arc:

| Character | Archetype | Role in story |
|---|---|---|
| **The Chief** *(name TBD)* | Driller / pragmatic leader | Skeptical-but-fair authority. Gates the trust progression. Becomes political/diplomatic ally for downriver expansion. |
| **The Shaman** *(name TBD)* | Most invested in Mani knowledge | Player's **first true ally.** Social bridge into the tribe, lore source, accompanies player on key story missions. Possibly a permanent companion. |
| **The Scout** *(name TBD)* | The first Grinder you save | Personal stake-holder. Their survival is the seed of the entire trust arc. Could become a companion or quest-giver. |

Other ~12–17 Grinders are generic — variants of the four archetypes (Scout/Driller/Chief/Shaman) with equipment/color/scale variation. **3 named + ~15 generic** keeps casting tight.

> **Open call to lock:** 2 named (Chief + Shaman only), 3 named (above), or 4 named (one per archetype, adds Driller). Recommendation = 3.

## 5. Where the First Refinement Ceremony Happens

> **Open call to lock.** Three options:

### Option A — Headwaters Accord outpost *(proposed default)*
Player and Shaman travel to the abandoned outpost; the equipment is there. Then bring the crystal back to camp to present.
- **Two distinct beats** (private demonstration + public reveal) with strong emotional structure
- Reuses the wake-site location, deepening its meaning
- Mild fast-travel friction the first time, justified by story

### Option B — Grinder camp itself
Player constructs makeshift refinement equipment at the camp from salvage.
- Single beat. Less travel.
- Less dramatic, tighter pacing.
- Loses the "the outpost held a secret all along" reveal.

### Option C — Lithic Mow drill site
First refinement happens during the reclamation assault. Combines Phases 3 and 4.
- Most cinematic ("we made it together, in the place that built the old world")
- Most complex to author — has to handle combat + ceremony + revelation in one sequence

## 6. How "Player Knowledge" Works Mechanically

The player's pre-Breach knowledge is represented through **crafting as a gating mechanic**:

- **Only the player can refine Mani** at game start
- Each Mega Structure reclamation unlocks the next refinement tier for the player
- After Phase 3 (first refinement ceremony), **the player teaches Grinders** — represented mechanically by Grinder colonists being able to *operate* refinement stations the player has set up (passive crafting in the background while the player raids)
- Dialogue throughout reflects knowledge — Disco-Elysium-light, where the player has "I recognize this" options on certain interactions

**Knowledge is the gating power**, not stats or stamina. Beautifully integrates the humanist hero framing with gameplay.

## 7. Side Quest Hooks (post-Allied tier)

Once Allied, the Grinders open up additional content:

- **The Lost Hunter** — a Grinder party went missing in the Riverwild months ago. Recover their bones (or find them as a degraded survivor / cryo-survivor unrelated to the Accord caste).
- **The Old Cache** — Shaman knows of a pre-Breach cache her grandmother spoke of. Helping her find it unlocks early Bhu-Mani recipes.
- **The Wild Driller** — a rival Grinder splinter group rejected the player's knowledge as heresy. Diplomacy or combat resolution.
- **The Spoiled Vein** — a Mani vein in the valley is unusually corrupted (Bleed pool). Investigating reveals a fragment of Accord history.

These are optional, scope-flexible, and reuse existing systems.

---

## 8. Why This Arc Carries the Prototype

In the standalone prototype, the trust arc IS the campaign. The prototype's content arc:
1. Wake → tutorial
2. Save the Scout → enter camp (Wary)
3. Mini-quests → First Refinement (Allied)
4. Joint reclamation of Lithic Mow → (Tribal Kin)
5. Hint of the wider world (downriver glimpse, audio log foreshadowing The Bleed and the conspiracy)

A complete 10–20 hour standalone experience that ends on the cusp of the main game's larger journey. Players who love the prototype want to play the main game to see what happens at Vats and Forge.
