
# Grinder Trust Arc

The prototype's primary social mechanic and the main game's act-one narrative spine. The player starts as a stranger to a semi-hostile tribe and ends as tribal kin sharing knowledge. The arc is **earned through demonstration**, not handed over via quest completion.

> See [[11 Factions and Species#Grinders (Humans — primary prototype faction)|Grinder roster]] for the four archetypes. See [[13 Campaign Structure#5 Story Acts|Act 1: Trust]] for where this fits in the campaign.

---

## 1. Design Goals

1. **The Grinders should feel like people, not quest-givers.** A small tribe (~15–20) where you remember individual faces.
2. **Trust is the gating mechanism for everything Lithic Mow has to offer** — camp access, vendor, crafting, companions.
3. **The Drill Assault is the prototype's load-bearing sequence** — combat, story revelation, mechanical unlock, and social transformation all converging in one cinematic operation.

---

## 2. The Three-Phase Arc

### Phase 1 — Save the Scout (~15–30 min)
- Wake at the Headwaters cryo facility. **Player retains cryo-memory of Bhu-Mani refinement** — fragmented memories, but enough to refine it on Accord equipment.
- Brief tutorial in the abandoned outpost.
- Venture out. Encounter a lone Grinder **Scout** being attacked by **hostile scavengers** — a rival Grinder splinter group (the same heretic faction the Elder Chieftain later leads). Establishes early that the Grinders are fractured, not unified.
- **First combat in the world** = saving the Scout. Mechanically teaches the player how combat works while narratively earning the first sliver of trust.
- Wounded Scout reluctantly leads you toward the camp.
- **Trust tier → WARY.** Camp access, basic dialogue, no vendor, no crafting station, no quests beyond "don't die in our space."

> Note: there are **no Husks at Lithic Mow** and **no wildlife anywhere** (cut — Topic 9). Threats in the valley are hostile rival Grinders / scavengers and environmental Bleed-pool hazards. Named-Husk encounters begin at Genesis Vats. The player's pre-existing Bhu-Mani knowledge is the reason they can lead the Trust Arc's climactic Demonstration in Phase 3 without needing a Husk teacher.

### Phase 2 — Prove and Prepare (~1–2 hrs)
- Settle into the camp. **Chief** receives you: *"You can stay. We're watching you."*
- Witness Grinders using **raw Mani as grenades** — random effects, occasional self-harm, losing tribe members to their own ignorance.
- Handful of "prove useful" missions: defend camp from a rival-scavenger raid, recover stolen supplies, heal an injured Grinder. Each builds practical trust without changing the tier.
- The **Shaman** is the most curious about the player — asks questions, watches closely. Becomes the player's social bridge into the tribe.
- Chief eventually proposes the unthinkable: **the tribe's biggest operation in generations.** Reactivate the long-silent Lithic Mow drill. The Shaman has dreamed of it. The Chief sees a chance to break the cycle of dying-to-ignorance. The Scout will come because they owe the player. The plan is set.
- *(Trust tier stays WARY through this phase — the watershed is the assault itself.)*

### Phase 3 — The Drill Assault *(climactic sequence)*
The prototype's single most important sequence. Has two internal trust-shifts.

**Approach & breach**
- Joint operation with Chief, Shaman, and Scout. Player + Grinder squad approach the silent drill.
- Clear initial hazards (rival scavengers, environmental Bleed pools at the entrance). No Husks in the valley.
- Push into the drill structure itself — Accord-era mining halls, dust-covered, dormant for millennia.

**Mid-assault: The Demonstration** *(watershed beat)*
- Reach an old Accord refinement chamber inside the drill. The Shaman recognizes the equipment from oral history but has no idea what it does.
- A wave of corruption attacks. The Shaman pulls out a raw Mani shard, about to throw it as a grenade.
- Player intervenes: *"Wait. Watch."*
- Player demonstrates **Bhu-Mani refinement** on the spot using the Accord chamber's equipment — drawing on the cryo-memory they've carried since waking.
- **First Bhu-Mani crystal forms** in the player's hand. Shaman is transfigured. Chief witnesses. Scout exhales.
- Combat resumes — now with refined Bhu-Mani as a tool. The squad pushes deeper.
- **Trust tier → ALLIED.** Mid-sequence unlocks: vendor opens (in-camp on return), crafting station available, blueprints accessible, Grinder colonist recruitment begins. Functionally the player gains Allied benefits the moment they return to camp after the assault completes.

**Final push: Mass Production** *(bonding climax)*
- Push to the drill's core. Reactivate the drill machinery in a layered mini-objective sequence.
- Drill comes back online. Within hours it begins extracting raw Mani at scale.
- The squad returns to camp triumphant. The whole tribe gathers. The first refined Bhu-Mani crystals are distributed.
- Chief: *"Today the Mow speaks for us again. Today, ignorance dies."*
- **Trust tier → TRIBAL KIN.** Companions in combat unlock (Scout joins as first combat companion). Tribe lore unlocks. Base co-building begins. Full Grinder gear available.

## 3. Trust Tiers — three discrete levels

Story-gated rather than reputation-numerical. Two discrete tier-shifts both happen during the Drill Assault — no Skyrim-style grind, no slow-creep.

| Tier | Unlocks | How to reach |
|---|---|---|
| **Wary** (start) | Camp access, basic dialogue, "prove useful" mini-quests | Save the Scout in Phase 1 |
| **Allied** | Vendor, crafting station, blueprints, Grinder colonist recruitment | The Demonstration beat (mid-Phase 3) |
| **Tribal Kin** | Combat companions (Scout joins first), tribe lore reveals, base co-building, full Grinder gear unlocks | Mass Production beat (end of Phase 3) |

## 4. Named Grinder NPCs

Three named individuals carry the arc. Other ~12–17 Grinders are generic — variants of the four archetypes (Scout / Driller / Chief / Shaman) with equipment / color / scale variation.

| Character | Archetype | Role across the arc |
|---|---|---|
| **The Chief** *(name TBD)* | Driller / pragmatic leader | Gates the trust progression. Approves you for camp in Phase 1. Proposes the Drill Assault in Phase 2. Witnesses the Demonstration in Phase 3. Becomes political/diplomatic ally for downriver expansion in main game. |
| **The Shaman** *(name TBD)* | Most invested in Mani knowledge | Player's **first true ally** and lore-source. Builds bridge during Phase 2. Recognizes the Accord chamber in Phase 3. **The Demonstration beat happens because of her.** Possible permanent companion in later expeditions. |
| **The Scout** *(name TBD)* | The first Grinder you save | Personal stake-holder from Phase 1. Comes on the Drill Assault out of debt. **Becomes the player's first combat companion** once Tribal Kin tier is reached — a persistent presence in raids going forward. |

## 5. How "Player Knowledge" Works Mechanically

The player's pre-Breach knowledge is represented through **crafting as a gating mechanic**:

- **Only the player can refine Mani** at game start.
- Each Mega Structure reclamation unlocks the next refinement tier for the player.
- After the Demonstration beat in Phase 3, **the player teaches Grinder colonists** — represented mechanically by Grinder colonists being able to *operate* refinement stations the player has set up (passive crafting in the background while the player raids).
- Dialogue throughout reflects knowledge — Disco-Elysium-light, where the player has "I recognize this" options on certain interactions.

**Knowledge is the gating power**, not stats or stamina. Beautifully integrates the humanist-hero framing with gameplay.

## 6. Side Quests (post-Allied tier)

Once Allied, the Grinders open up additional content. Optional, scope-flexible, reuse existing systems.

- **The Lost Hunter** — a Grinder party went missing in the Riverwild months ago. Recover their bones (or find a degraded survivor).
- **The Old Cache** — Shaman knows of a pre-Breach cache her grandmother spoke of. Helping her find it unlocks early Bhu-Mani recipe upgrades.
- **The Wild Driller** — a rival Grinder splinter group rejects the player's knowledge as heresy. Diplomacy or combat resolution. Possible seed for the **Elder Chieftain** boss in main game.
- **The Spoiled Vein** — a Mani vein in the valley is unusually corrupted (Bleed pool). Investigating reveals a fragment of Accord history.

---

## 7. Why the Drill Assault Carries the Prototype

In the standalone prototype, **the Drill Assault is the climactic sequence the entire experience builds toward.** Every prior raid teaches you systems; every prior mini-quest builds your standing with the tribe; the assault is where all of it pays off in a single converging operation.

Authoring discipline: this sequence needs more iteration and polish than any other content in the prototype. It carries:
- Combat showcase (large encounter, multiple archetypes, environmental hazards)
- The story revelation (the Demonstration)
- The mechanical unlock (refined Bhu-Mani enters the game)
- The social transformation (two trust-tier shifts)
- The emotional payoff (tribal celebration, the first true cooperation between player and Grinders)

A complete 10–20 hour prototype builds momentum toward this sequence. Players who love it want to play the main game to see what happens at Vats and Forge.
